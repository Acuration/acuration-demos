December 23, 2011
Suzlon announces 166 MW (~Rs. 935 cr.) of new orders in India
Cumulative order wins between October 22 to December 22, 2011, excluding separatelyannounced ordersProduct mix of primarily S82-1.5 MW and S88-2.1 MW turbinesStrong customer mix covering PSUs, large corporates and SMEsRepeat customers account for over half of new ordersPune: Suzlon Energy Limited (SEL), the worlds fifth* leading and Indias largest wind turbinemanufacturer, today announced cumulative orders of 166.30 MW in India over a two month period,excluding orders announced separately.These cover various firm orders secured between October 22 and December 22, 2011 and represent astrong customermix, covering PSUs, large corporates and SMEs with order sizes ranging from as small as0.6 MW to 20 MW.Mr. Tulsi Tanti  founder, Chairman and Managing Director, Suzlon Group, said: These ordersunderscore the vibrant and rapidly growing Indian wind sector, and our ability to tailor end-to-end windsolutions to all customers. With an ever increasing need for energy, along with technology making itpossible to profitably harness low wind sites, we see India continuing to be one of our fastest growingmarkets in the years ahead.Some of the key projects include:Malpani Group: A renowned business conglomerate in the state of Maharashtra with business interest inagro, leisure, real estate, entertainment, farming, finance, transport and tea, in addition to wind energy.The group has a 79.85 MW of wind power projects operating across Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh,Karnataka and Rajasthan, and the latest order takes its portfolio to over 100 MW. The Malpani group hasbeen Suzlons customer for more than a decade.Rajasthan Gums: A leading supplier of gaur gum headquartered in Jodhpur, Rajasthan. The company hassteadily built its wind energy portfolio, and has projects of 30 MW already operational, and adds 15.6MW with its latest order. Rajasthan Gums has been Suzlons customer since 2005.Gujarat Power Corporation Ltd: GPCL owns and operates power plants based on different fuels acrossGujarat, and adds a new order of 10.5 MW to its existing 6 MW project in Kutch, Gujarat. GPCL has beenSuzlons customer since 2010.Sterling Agro Group: A leading name in the dairy products industry, the Sterling Agro Group has a windportfolio of 21.55 MW operating across Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh and Karnataka, this order takes theirportfolio to nearly 29 MW. Sterling Agro has been Suzlons customer since 2010.India is the worlds fifth largest wind market with a cumulative installed base of over 15,000 MW. Thetotal estimated onshore wind power potential in India, as per the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy(MNRE), stands at over 49,000 MW. The sector is poised for accelerated growth driven by demand forpower, strong support for renewables by the Governments sound policy framework and a positiveinvestment outlook.
