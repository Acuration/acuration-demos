RES will continue its work on the site in County of Forty Mile No. 8 as Balance of Plant construction contractor, with ACCIONA Energía planning to initiate operations in early 2025. The largest RES-developed project in Canada, Forty Mile will be constructed in two phases: Phase 1 will feature 49 turbines for a total generating capacity of approximately 280MW, with the option for Phase 2 with 21 turbines with an additional capacity of 120MW. At its full 400MW capacity, Forty Mile would be Canada’s largest wind farm, generating enough energy to power nearly 230,000 Albertan homes. The project will employ approximately 250 people during peak construction later this year. “RES has been at the forefront of energy transition for more than 40 years, developing, constructing and operating assets that continue to expand access to affordable zero-carbon energy. The Forty Mile wind project is a significant energy transition milestone for the county, the province and Canada,” said John Rohde, CEO, RES Americas. “We appreciate the strong support from the County of Forty Mile and the community throughout the development, and we’re now ready to move into construction alongside ACCIONA Energía.” RES and ACCIONA Energía will work closely with stakeholders, including local governmental agencies and landowners to ensure a smooth transition and to maximize the positive impacts of the project on the community. “This acquisition aligns with our long-term view of expanding ACCIONA Energía’s renewable portfolio and underscores our commitment to a greener, more sustainable future,” said Joaquin Castillo, CEO of ACCIONA North America. “The project not only strengthens our operational footprint but also reinforces our commitment to local communities. Through our Social Impact Management program, ACCIONA Energía will reinvest a portion of its annual revenue to support education, wellness and environmental stewardship programs in the local community.” The main energy and environmental attribute supply offtake from Forty Mile will go to Cenovus Energy, a Canadian-based integrated energy company, via a Virtual Power Purchase Agreement (VPPA) as part of its efforts to offset a portion of its electricity-related emissions. The balance of supply from the project will contribute clean energy to Alberta’s merchant power and carbon offset markets. Federal programs such as the Smart Renewables and Electrification Pathways (SREP) program and the Clean Technology Investment Tax Credit (ITC) and Alberta’s Technology Innovation and Emissions Reduction (TIER) regulation play a significant role in driving the development of and investments in renewable energy projects such as Forty Mile. “These federal and provincial programs help drive the development of renewable energy sources by encouraging private-sector investment, driving job creation and stimulating local economies in Canada,” said Rohde. Forty Mile is the seventh project RES has developed in Alberta and its 23 rd in Canada. It builds on the success of the adjacent 125MW Rattlesnake Ridge wind project, also developed by RES. Over the past 20 years, RES has been involved in the development of 3.2GW of wind and solar projects and overseen the construction of nearly 1.8GW of wind, solar, transmission and storage projects in Canada. The company continues to develop utility-scale wind, solar and battery storage projects across the country.
