October 11, 2011
 Suzlon wins second Sri Lanka order
Repeat order from Sri Lankan conglomerate Senok GroupOrder for 10 units of the S88 - 2.1 MW turbine, aggregating to 21 MWNew order takes Senoks wind portfolio to over 31 MWPune: Suzlon Energy Limited (SEL), worlds fifth largest* and Indias largest wind turbinemanufacturer, won a repeat order from Senok to supply 10 units of Suzlons S88 - 2.1 MWwind turbine generators, aggregating to 21 MW of wind power capacity. The project will becommissioned in two phases and be completed by May 2012.Speaking on the order Mr Noel Selvanayagam, President, Senok Group, said: This neworder underlines Senok Groups strategic focus on wind energy. We take pride in being thepioneer in commercial wind power projects in Sri Lanka. We are happy to once againpartner with Suzlon for this new project and count on Suzlons collaborative approach andtime tested service capabilities to continue our endeavor towards a greener tomorrow.Mr Tulsi R. Tanti, Chairman and Managing Director, Suzlon Group, said: This repeatorder underlines Suzlons strong value proposition - offering expertise and flexible solutions- from concept to commissioning  to meet our customers unique needs across diversegeographies and wind regimes. This island nation has considerable potential for wind, and agreat need for power to drive its economic growth. We at Suzlon look forward to buildinggreat synergies in partnership with the Senok Group in Sri Lanka.Suzlon has previously supplied a 10 MW wind project to the Senok Group, the order markedkey firsts for Sri Lankas wind energy sector  as the first project to feature megawatt classturbines, and also the first project by any private developer in the island nation. The new 21MW project will come up alongside the existing project site in the Puttalm district of SriLanka.Senok Group, is a diversified business conglomerate of Sri Lanka with interests acrossconstruction machinery, automotives, infrastructure development, tea, haulage and mining.The Group has an established presence in the clean energy space with its wind portfolio andinvestments in mini hydro projects.
