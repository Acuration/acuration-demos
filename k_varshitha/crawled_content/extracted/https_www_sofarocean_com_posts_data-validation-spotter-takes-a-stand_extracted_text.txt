12m wave height a periodic  or sinusoidal  wave is the most basic wave form we have although such a wave never actually occurs in the ocean you could think of it as an archetype for a very narrowband swell field traveling in from a remote storm due to its simplicity the periodic wave is widely used as validation of wave sensors and it is certainly a good starting point the agreement between the teststand and spotter measurements for the periodic wave motion is excellent see figure 1 both for the horizontal displacements top panel and vertical displacements bottom panel the correlation between the stand motion and spotter data is 099 and 098 for the horizontal and vertical displacement time series respectively â however ocean waves are never purely periodic and a more realistic
