Why are ocean currents important?

There are two types of ocean currents: surface currents and deep water currents. Deepwater currents make up 90% of the ocean and refer to water that is driven around the oceans by density (temperature and salinity) and gravity. Surface currents make up the other 10% of water in the ocean. These currents move throughout the upper 400 meters of the ocean.

Surface currents are typically wind-driven which means water moves both vertically and horizontally. Horizontal surface currents are those which we experience regularly: things like rip currents, longshore currents, and tidal currents.
