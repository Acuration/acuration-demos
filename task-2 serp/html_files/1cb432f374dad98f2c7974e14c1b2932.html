        SunSource Energy raises growth capital and provides exit to its existing investor via a strategic capital raise from SHV Energy NV

        10 May 2021 PDF
        Subject Deal alerts
        Categories Energy
        Jurisdictions India
        • Link copied

        EY has successfully advised SunSource Energy in raising growth capital and providing exit to its exiting investor, NEEV through a strategic capital raise from SHV Energy NV.

        EY acted as the exclusive financial advisor to SunSource Energy and its shareholders.

        Founded in January 2010 by Adarsh Das and Kushagra Nandan, SunSource Energy is a solar project developer and an engineering, procurement and construction (EPC) service provider. It is a distributed solar power company focused on commercial and industrial segments through rooftop and ground-mounted projects. It has over 300 solar power projects and is currently operating from across India, Philippines, Thailand, Singapore, and USA. The company also develops and invests in off-site commercial and industrial projects known as open access solar projects. 

        Head quartered in Hoofddorp (Netherlands) SHV Energy is a wholly owned subsidiary of SHV, a family-owned multinational, that consists of a group of specialised energy companies. SHV Energy provides low-carbon and clean energy solutions to business and residential customers in over 25 countries across four continents. It is a leading global distributor of off-grid energy including LPG, small-scale LNG and biofuels through its brands, including Calor, Ipragaz, Liquigas, Primagaz, Supergasbras, Supergas and EM3. The company has over 17,000 employees and serves more than 30 million customers globally. 

        As energy majors globally set out to achieve zero carbon targets, India continues to be one of the focus markets as it that provides access to large capacities and capabilities to set up additional capacity. SHV Energy’s investment in SunSource is based on the same thesis. EY was able to leverage on its strong sector expertise and global network of strategic and financial investors to garner significant interest in the transaction and create a win-win solution for both sides.
        Srishti Ahuja Taneja
        Partner, Investment Banking Advisory, EY
