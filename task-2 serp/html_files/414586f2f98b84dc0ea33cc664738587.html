    How SunSource Energy emerged as one of the key players in India's solar energy space

    Synopsis

    SunSource Energy is one of the key players in India's solar space with 60 completed projects with a cumulative capacity of 17 MW across 12 states.

    By Preeti Verma Lal

    Bathed in the artificial yellow light of a New Delhi restaurant, the dainty dumplings lay unattended. Not a ray of sunshine was peering through the cracks but my mind was getting fuzzy with the zeros of the sunlight. At any given moment, the sun emits 174,000,000,000,000,000, or 174 quadrillion watts. Phew!

    The solar energy hitting the earth exceeds the total energy consumed by humanity by a factor of over 20,000 times. If one were to harness solar energy, the purest form of renewable energy, it would mean zero greenhouse emissions and lower electricity bills. All one needs to do is mount sleek, siliconlaced solar panels on the rooftop at a 260 tilt due south. And let the sun be an eternal power supplier. The fat electricity bills will thin. And it will save the world some warming woes.

    Feasible Reality Sounds like sci-fi stuff? No. It is feasible reality. Bathed in artificial yellow light, I was listening intently to two Massachusetts-trained solar engineers who, after long years in the US, returned home to set up SunSource Energy, a Noida-headquartered company, which develops, engineers, procures, constructs and operates solar power plants and projects. Adarsh Das, chief executive of SunSource, was scribbling on a notepad, explaining net metering, the solar energy S-curve, generation-transmission-distribution intricacies and the government's plan to up the solar contribution to 20% of the country's energy needs.

    Facts were bouncing off my head. With a bachelor's degree from IIT, an MBA from Michigan and a master's from Massachusetts, Das knows his sun-mathematics perfect. Kushagra Nandan, SunSource's president, is driven by the dream to replicate â€” perhaps, better â€” the solar energy growth curve that he watched unfold during his stints with global solar majors like SunPower and GE.

    For Nandan and Das, it all started more than six years ago when the entrepreneurial adrenaline kicked in. The road was rough. And naysayers many. Das's father thought his bright son had sadly lost his marbles. For the moneybags, the idea of solar energy was an alien money-making idea.

    Gigantic firms were not convinced about the viability of how solar panels could halve their electricity bills. Many cried hoarse about proof of concept. It took the two 30-somethings six months to get the first solar energy project nod. Today, SunSource Energy is one of the key players in India's solar space with 60 completed projects with a cumulative capacity of 17 MW (built and worked on) across 12 states, including one of Indiaâ€™s first rooftop solar public private partnership projects.

    Burning Bright For global market, SunSource Energy has designed and engineered solar projects with a cumulative capacity of 900 MW. In New Delhi, the biggies are biting into the solar pie; solar panels are lined on rooftops of India Habitat Centre, Vasant Valley School, Max Bupa office, among others. Right now, Das and Nandan's hands are full with projects in the US and Southeast Asia. As if to walk the talk, Nandan's house runs on solar energy and Das installed solar panels at his parents' home in Patna.

    SunSource Energy does not focus on solar market segments such as solar lanterns and street lights. However, it has designed and implemented a project related to water pumping and purification with solar energy for SANA, a non-profit organisation, and received the Google Impact Challenge Award.

    To pedal-push India's solar energy future, Sun Source is also the authorised channel partner of the ministry of new and renewable energy. With net metering now a reality in India, the average Joe can also make or save a few dimes by harnessing solar energy. As a concept, net metering allows consumers (people, companies, buildings) to export power to the grid and be compensated for the exported power.

    The government is prepped to ride the renewable energy wave, setting a target of 1,00,000 MW of solar power by 2022. According to MakeinIndia.com, India's installed solar capacity has increased from barely 20 MW in 2011 to 3.74 GW as on March 30, 2015, and hopes to add 10.86 GW of utility-scale solar and grid-connected rooftop solar capacity by 2016-17.

    With such growth drivers, Sun-Source Energy is acing its skills and upping its solar aspirations. In a short span of time, SunSource Energy has grown from a two-man army into a 45-employee company with current valuation at Rs 25 crore; it wants to join the Rs 100-crore club by the end of 2016. At lunch with Nandan and Das, I was so caught up in solar mathematics that everything else was redundant.

    The dainty dumplings were sinking in the soup. I was chewing on the hope of the two solar engineers that in our lifetime the roof of the common man will also be topped with solar panels. The world will shun fossil fuels and embrace solar energy. The nonpolluting silicon-laced solar panels will not only lower electricity bills, it will also save the world's lungs rotting with pollutants and carbon emissions.

    (The author is a freelance writer and photographer)
